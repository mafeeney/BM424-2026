At the previous emergency pandemic response team meeting, the following action points were agreed:

  • Deploy CRISPR/Cas12a and Metagenomic Sequencing for Rapid Diagnosis

  • Genomic Epidemiology to Trace Outbreak Origins

  • Risk-Stratified Prophylaxis with Stewardship Monitoring

  • Implement Public Health Measures: Use FFP2 masks and implement quarantines.

  • Medical Supply Distribution: Implement tracking systems, create stockpiles

Pandemic Report

Briefing:

  • Based on collated hospital reports and death certificates, it is believed that there have been 39 deaths due to anthrax in Ardkeld thus far

  • There are currently 365 patients in the Riverview with confirmed anthrax, or symptoms consistent with anthrax

  • There are currently 10 patients currently in the ICU with confirmed anthrax

  • Genomic epidemiology of all samples isolated from patients indicates that they come from the same source.

  • An antibiotic stewardship programme has been approved by the local NHS board and has been implemented.

  • A medical supply provision and tracking system has been designed and implemented.

  • A proposal for use of new CRISPR technologies in diagnosis has been submitted to the local NHS board.

News/blog post #1

Increasing Concerns Over Outbreak of anthrax in Ardkeld
Ardkeld Express

The situation appears to be quite grim in Ardkeld, as we see cases mount and the city struggles under the wave of another pandemic, coming hard on the heels of the COVID pandemic which began nearly five years ago.

To date there have been 39 deaths attributed to the pathogen, Bacillus anthracis, and hundreds of people are currently ill with the disease.

The city’s Emergency Pandemic Response Committee is working to pin down the source of the outbreak, but there are real fears for what impact this new pandemic will have on the city’s economy. The spectre of COVID looms large over the residents of this lovely city.

The EPRC have proposed the use of a novel CRISPR/Cas12a and Metagenomic Sequencing method for rapid diagnosis, but this technique does not yet have NHS approval and there are concerns over the potential false positive and false negative rates of this new technique.

The Emergency Pandemic Response Committee was not available for comment. The First Minister of Scotland made a statement saying that every effort possible is being made to tackle the pandemic in Ardkeld.

News/blog post #5

BBC Scotland
anthrax pandemic in Ardkeld
by Edith Rogers
09 March, 2025

Hard on the heels of the COVID-19 pandemic and a number of other health scares (monkeypox, or “mpox”, avian flu, and worries about other infectious disease outbreaks), another pandemic has hit the Scottish town of Ardkeld. This time, it’s Ardkeld, a bacterium that can very sadly be fatal.

There have been 39 deaths in the pandemic so far, but perhaps the most frightening aspect to this new pandemic is rumours that these Bacillus anthracis strains may have become resistant to even more antibiotics - a truly chilling possibility.

Scientists have been warning about the antibiotic resistance crisis for years, saying that careless use of antibiotics could mean that these precious medicines will no longer be available to us in the future.

Some of the Bacillus anthracis strains found in Ardkeld are reportedly resistant to all clinically available antibiotics, and scientists warn that these drug-resistant pathogens might be able to spread to new locations. Will nearby towns be next, or could these Bacillus anthracis strains go as global as the SARS-COV2 virus did?
